Nipha Virus News:-

"Over 250 isolated, 11 with symptoms" says Kerala Health Minister Veena George.A 12 year old boy died of Nipah virus infection on September 5. Health Minister said "of the 251 contacts, 54 are high-risk contacts out of which 30 are healthcare workers. This includes parents and few close relatives of the child too".She also said "the special laboratory set up at the medical college hospital by the Pune NIV team will test the samples". An area of a three-kilometer radius from the house of the deceased child has been declared as a containment zone.As per the WHO, Nipha Virus infected people initially develop symptoms that include fever, headaches, myalgia, vomiting, and sore throat.This can be followed by dizziness, drowsiness, altered consciousness, and neurological signs that indicate acute encephalitis.Some even experience atypical pneumonia and severe respiratory problems, including acute respiratory distress. Encephalitis and seizures occur in severe cases, progressing to coma within 24 to 48 hours.
